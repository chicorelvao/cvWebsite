Development of a 3D Tactile Sensor

Internship
Silicon Austria Labs
Villach, Austria
2022/23 - 6 Months

I got my first job while finishing my master's in order to write my thesis. I worked on magnetic 3D tactile sensors for an Austrian research institute with the objective of creating buttons that could detect complex three-dimensional touch patterns, unlike regular buttons or touchpads, to control the movement of vehicles or to provide tactile sensation to robots. The institute had tried to design these sensors before, but the results were lacking. To validate the design of sensors, Mumax3, a simple micromagnetic simulator, was used. To ease the building of complex simulations, I made an OOP Python library that used the Mumax3 simple scripting language but allowed for a more abstract design of simulations, making them closer to reality and thus easier to validate my sensor designs. Additionally, I also built a data pipeline to connect different programs and speed data analysis. In the end, we successfully designed a 3D tactile button using anisotropic magnetoresistive sensors. Compared to the previous attempted designs, I improved the detection volume from 0.1x0.1x0.1 mm to 0.7x0.7x3 mm while keeping the measurement errors below 0.05 mm.
